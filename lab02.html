Assignment 2

1. The length & breadth of a rectangle and radius of a circle are input through the keyboard. Write a program to calculate the area & perimeter of the rectangle, and the area & circumference of the circle.

Sample Input:

Enter length of rectangle: 10
Enter breadth of rectangle: 5
Enter radius of circle: 7

Expected Output:

Area of rectangle: 50.00
Perimeter of rectangle: 30.00
Area of circle: 153.94
Circumference of circle: 43.98

2. Two numbers are input through the keyboard into two locations C and D. Write a program to interchange the contents of C and D.

Sample Input:

Enter value for C: 10
Enter value for D: 20

Expected Output:

Before interchange: C = 10, D = 20
After interchange: C = 20, D = 10

3. If a five-digit number is input through the keyboard, write a program to calculate the sum of its digits. (Hint: Use the modulus operator '%')

Sample Input:

Enter a five-digit number: 12345

Expected Output:

Sum of digits: 15

4. If a five-digit number is input through the keyboard, write a program to reverse the number.

Sample Input:

Enter a five-digit number: 54321

Expected Output:

Reversed number: 12345

5. If a four-digit number is input through the keyboard, write a program to obtain the sum of the first and last digit of this number.

Sample Input:

Enter a four-digit number: 7892

Expected Output:

Sum of first and last digit: 9

6. In a town, the percentage of men is 52. The percentage of total literacy is 48. If total percentage of literate men is 35% of the total population, write a program to find the total number of illiterate men and women if the population of the town is 80,000.

Problem Data:

Total Population: 80,000
Percentage of men: 52%
Total literacy: 48%
Literate men: 35% of total population

Expected Output:

Illiterate Men: 13600
Illiterate Women: 28000

7. A cashier is equipped with currency notes in denominations of ₹10, ₹50, and ₹100. When a withdrawal amount is entered via keyboard—expressed in hundreds (e.g., 5 for ₹500)—determine how many notes of each denomination the cashier must dispense to fulfill the requested amount.

Sample Input:

Enter amount to be withdrawn (in hundreds): 18

Expected Output:

Amount: 1800
Number of 100 rupee notes: 18
Number of 50 rupee notes: 0
Number of 10 rupee notes: 0

8. If the total selling price of 15 items and the total profit earned on them is input through the keyboard, write a program to find the cost price of one item.

Sample Input:

Enter total selling price of 15 items: 2250
Enter total profit: 750

Expected Output:

Cost price of one item: 100.00

9. If a five-digit number is input through the keyboard, write a program to print a new number by adding one to each of its digits.

Sample Input:

Enter a five-digit number: 12391

Expected Output:

New number: 23402

10. Write a program to output whether a person should be insured or not, his/her premium rate and maximum policy amount for which he/she can be insured based on the following rules.

Insurance Rules:

If a person’s health is excellent and the person is between 25 and 35 years of age and lives in a city and is male then the premium is Rs.4 per thousand and his policy amount cannot exceed Rs.2 lakhs.

If a person satisfies all the above conditions except that the sex is female, then the premium is Rs.3 per thousand and her policy amount cannot exceed Rs. 1 lakh.

If a person’s health is poor and the person is between 25 and 35 years of age and lives in a village and is male then the premium amount is Rs. 6 per thousand and his policy cannot exceed Rs. 10,000.

In all other cases, the person is not insured.

Sample Input 1:

Health: excellent, Age: 30, Location: city, Sex: male

Expected Output 1:

Status: Insured
Premium Rate: Rs. 4 per thousand
Maximum Policy Amount: Rs. 2,00,000

Sample Input 2:

Health: poor, Age: 40, Location: city, Sex: female

Expected Output 2:

Status: Not Insured
